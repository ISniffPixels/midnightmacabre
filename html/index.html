midnight horror

When I was a kid, I used to run to my bed as fast as I could, every time I turned the light switch off in my bedroom. Middle of the night trips to the restroom were even more terrifying. Balling up my tiny, shaky, fists, I expected the boogeyman to sweep me away into the darkness, never to be seen again. The first ever horror movie I recall watching was Wes Craven's A Nightmare on Elm Street. I can't begin to count the amount of wide-eyed sleepless nights I had thanks to this iconic piece of film history. It baffled me how something so terrifying to me, could also be so mesmerizing, I couldn't stop watching. My life would eventually embark on a focused fascination with horror movies, and horror culture in general. Please, join me in celebrating horror by enjoying the content on this website. I hope you enjoy and are entertained by the dark, bloody, and creepy, just as much as I enjoy bringing it to you. Keep your eyes open, tread carefully, and may the fates have mercy on your soul....P.S. turn the lights on, if you're afraid of the dark....

Featured Video Game

One of the most interesting aspects of Dying Light is the day/night cycles. During the day, you can explore the open world and scavenge supplies. You can explore the city looking for loot, side quests, and other activities. You can also use the day to strengthen your character by visiting the safe houses and upgrading your skills. During the day, the city is full of life. You'll see people going about their daily business, working at shops, and driving cars. But as the day turns to night, things change. The city falls into chaos as hordes of ravenous zombies emerge from the shadows....read more
